Dear Reader

If you're reading this, it's likely I've sent it your way – but if you've stumbled upon it and find it pleasing, feel free to contact me.

Let's assume you are the intended reader and I am applying for a job at your organisation.

Why you should hire me

Well, first, I've sent you a lovely website which demonstrates some skill on my part. If that doesn't impress, let me tell you a bit about myself.

Who even is this guy?

I was 29 and it was 4am when I asked a developer friend how long it would take to become one. ‘A year’, he said – so, having programmed as a hobby (small scripts and Code Academy courses), I quit my job as an editor.

A year wasn't the best estimate – he's in software, so the scope crept.

Learning a new profession takes time and dedication. In the ensuing period, I partnered with my friend, working on anything I could get my hands on and soaking myself in all available wisdom for my new-chosen vocation.

I learned well.

Working in tech

I never thought I'd work in tech. I never thought I'd fall in love with tech – I studied philosophy and literature and English teaching before working as an editor.

But I did. It turns out making beautiful, clever or elegant things is very satisfying; challenging things even moreso.

Why you should hire me: part deux

  • Attention to detail
  • High standards of work
  • Organisational vision
  • Great lists

To my surprise, I think I'm quite good at this – and I'd like a chance to prove myself at your organisation, to the benefit of us both.
